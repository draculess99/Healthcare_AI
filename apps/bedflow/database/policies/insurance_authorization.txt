HOSPITAL POLICY: Insurance Authorization Delays

If a patient is clinically cleared for discharge but is facing an insurance authorization bottleneck, the patient should be flagged for Utilization Management (UM) review immediately. 
If the hospital's current bed occupancy is above 90% or ED boarding count exceeds 15, operations managers are authorized to escalate the case to the Chief Medical Officer (CMO) for an administrative peer-to-peer override to expedite the payer approval.
Patients with high discharge delay risk due to insurance should not have their bed marked as "pending cleaning" until the authorization is formally secured.
